How to Get There

Hit the Road from Manila:
Hop on a bus and enjoy a smooth ride to Subic for as low as ₱220. It takes around 3 and a half hours, making it the most budget-friendly way to get there.
Drive your own car and cruise down NLEX and SCTEX for a quicker trip—about 1 hour and 45 minutes. With fuel and tolls, expect to spend around ₱879 to ₱1,269.

Quick Trip from Ulo ng Apo:
Just a quick 2-minute taxi ride (₱70–₱85) or a short jeepney ride for only around ₱11–₱13, and you're already in Ayala Malls Harbor Point. Whether you're in a rush or want a more local experience, getting to Ayala is fast, easy, and affordable.
Harbor Point
Harbor Point, Subic: Harbor Point is a large shopping mall in Olongapo City, Philippines owned by property development firm Ayala Land, Inc., a real estate subsidiary of Ayala Corporation. It is the first Ayala Shopping Center located in the city of Olongapo and the second Mall to be opened in Central Luzon and outside Metro Manila.
What to Expect

Malls and Movie Theaters

Harbor Point is Ayala Land’s anchor development in the Subic Freeport Zone. This mall has become the leisure and shopping destination of choice for visitors and for residents, encompassing the City of Olongapo. The architecture is inspired by the maritime energy of Subic, with pointed roofs echoing the silhouette of sails, as in a regatta.
Treat yourself to shopping and entertainment at Ayala Malls Harbor Point, or enjoy the latest blockbuster movies at Harbor Point Cinemas. Harbor Point is a popular shopping destination in Olongapo, especially for travelers looking to stay nearby. Many visitors choose hotels within walking distance due to the mall's size and wide variety of stores and dining options.
During the Christmas season, Harbor Point becomes especially charming, with twinkling lights beautifully wrapped around plants and bushes, creating a festive and inviting ambiance.
