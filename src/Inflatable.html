How to Get There

Public Transportation:
Take a Victory Liner bus to Olongapo City (from Pasay, Cubao, or Caloocan). From the Olongapo terminal, head to the Blue Jeep Terminal near Chowking (Ulo ng Apo). Ride the jeep bound for Inflatable Island Beach Club and ask the driver to drop you off there.

Private Cars:
From Manila, take NLEX–SCTEX and exit at Subic. Inside Subic Bay Freeport Zone, turn right onto Rizal Highway. At the second stoplight, stay left and turn onto Burgos Street (beside Remy Field), then right to Canal Road. Continue to Dewey Avenue, then exit at Kalaklan Gate. Turn left onto RH National Highway and drive straight to Inflatable Island Beach Club.
Inflatable Island
Inflatable Island, located in Subic Bay, is the BIGGEST floating water playground in Asia. How big is big? At 3,400 sqm, it’s as big as 8 basketball courts put together side by side! In this exciting waterpark, guests can go down huge inflatable slides, swing on bridges, be catapulted on human launchers, and so much more!
What to Expect:

It’s a great place to bond with the family

Since the Inflatable Island is for everyone, whether young or old, you can be sure that everyone from your 50-year old aunt to your 10-year old niece is going to have a blast! For the kids and kids at heart, they can frolic on the inflatables for hours until all their energy is spent. For the folks who just need some vitamin sea without capering around on the inflatables, they can get in Samba Beach for only PHP 200 and have a nice swim.
Trampolines, monkey vines, jungle bars, and kiddie slides! Does the sound of those make you squeal in delight? You’ll definitely feel like a child again as you jump, bounce, and play on the inflatables! Now the tough part is this: with so many inflatables, where do you start?
You’ll have plenty to take photos of

If swimming or playing on the inflatables is not your thing, and you’d rather sit, relax, and take gorgeous photos, you’ll want to secure a spot at the Bali Lounge. The colorful bean bags and umbrellas is a good place to chill and chow, but be sure to go early because these relaxation stations are first come, first served.
